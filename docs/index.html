How it works — v3, calibrated to 4-decimal internal rating accuracy

The formula (doubles): mr = 0.61×(opp1+opp2−partner) + 0.39×your_rating + 0.74×gd_pct
Singles: mr = 0.61×opp + 0.39×your_rating + 0.13×gd_pct
Dynamic update: ra_new = 0.20×mr + 0.80×ra_prev

The opponent baseline carries 61% weight, your own current rating 39%. The score margin (games won − games lost) / total games is scaled by 0.74 for doubles and 0.13 for singles. The dynamic rating is a simple EMA with α=0.20. These parameters were optimised to reproduce the 4-decimal internal dynamic ratings shown on tennisrecord player profiles — Ravi's predicted final: 3.7233 vs PDF 3.7230; Pravin's: 3.8732 vs PDF 3.8729.

Your current rating is required — the formula uses it as a 39% component. Without it, the calculation falls back to opponent-only baseline (less accurate).
